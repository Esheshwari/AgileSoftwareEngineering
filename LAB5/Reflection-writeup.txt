The sprint planning exercise provided practical insight into capacity-based planning and risk management. 
The team defined a clear sprint goal focused on enabling core student functionalities such as registration, browsing, and enrollment.
Based on a total sprint capacity of 30 story points, user stories were selected carefully to avoid overloading the team. 
Risks such as technical dependencies, requirement uncertainty, and external integrations were identified early and documented.

The mid-sprint change simulation demonstrated the importance of protecting sprint scope. 
Instead of immediately accepting the new email notification requirement, the team evaluated its impact on capacity and sprint goal alignment. 
The decision to defer or reprioritize showed that Agile planning balances flexibility with commitment. 
Overall, the exercise highlighted that successful sprint execution depends on realistic planning, clear goals, risk awareness, and collaborative decision-making.
